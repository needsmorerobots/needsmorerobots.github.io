Uncanny uncitizens, we call them Fabrics and fashion them neatly into society.

New Humanity’s silent workforce, they’re the operators behind the walls. A periwinkle blue blur in our periphery.

Obedient, adaptable, inconspicuous.

Until now.

You see, today 6 androids self-terminated.

It’s your job to dive into their expired Brines and extract the truth about what happened.

is a post-cyberpunk puzzle game about diving into the minds of six androids to explore neural islands, collect lost memories, and surface information for an overbearing employer.

You play as Sheleri Fiss, a sort of brain surgeon for robots, which is significant because the game opens with a crisis demanding exactly her skillset. When six androids self-terminate on the same day, you’re tasked with carrying out a secret inquiry and corporate cover-up.

Your time with Neon is split between two distinct gameplay areas

Fiss’s personal workspace is an atmospheric android lab, 200 storeys up in Panarae’s looming headquarters. A two storey between-place that you start and end each day in. The Loom is always collecting and surfacing information about your game progress, it’s a place to build relationships, test faction conflicts, and broker bits of android truth.

Upstairs in the corner a Heliodeck keeps you in contact with NPCs and plays out like an in-world text adventure.

Downstairs the diving bell is your gateway to the brine.

THE
BRINE
DIVE
GAMEPLAY

Neural pathways that float like islands in the cognition waters of each Fabric mind. These intangible landscapes present puzzles for you to manipulate and explore. Each dive contains multiple layers where data cubes are hidden away and protected by guardians. Dense with analogue contraptions and glowing arcane glyphs, wherever you find yourself in the Brine the objective is always clear.

Find the data cubes, bring them back to the surface.

With Neon, we’re making a game where the world makes moves and the player gets to actively take stock. A game where narrative agency is about choosing how *your* protagonist internalises events rather than whether she behaves like a paragon or renegade to influence them.

A fixed fiction with room to react.

When the Archivist, Grand Overseer of the Notion of Knowing approaches you to collaborate, there won’t ever be path to change his vision for a posthuman future. But there will be opportunities to work -in counter- with the Wardens of Humankind whose transhuman agenda would benefit from your work.

It’s single-player experience about connections with others.

A robot game about humanity.

And a small sunset place to play with some big themes.
